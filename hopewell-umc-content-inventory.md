## SECTION 12: CONTENT TONE & VOICE GUIDELINES

### Voice Characteristics

**Hopewell's Voice**:
- **Warm and welcoming**: Friendly without being overly casual
- **Personal**: Uses "we" and "you"; Pastor Don's message is in first person
- **Inclusive**: "Every age and every level of maturity"
- **Humble**: "It's not about us!" - focus on service and Christ
- **Relational**: Emphasis on relationships and community
- **Authentic**: Real about spiritual journey ("grow in relationship")
- **Action-oriented**: Invitations to "take the next step" and "come meet you"

### Writing Style

**Do's**:
- Use contractions (we're, you're, can't)
- Keep paragraphs short (2-4 sentences)
- Use active voice
- Include clear calls-to-action
- Make information scannable with headers
- Be specific about times, locations, contacts
- End with invitation or next step

**Don'ts**:
- Avoid religious jargon without explanation
- Don't make assumptions about biblical knowledge
- Avoid being preachy or judgmental
- Don't hide important information (times, location)
- Avoid passive voice and vague language
- Don't use churchy insider language

### Example Comparisons

**Instead of**: "Join our congregation for Sunday corporate worship"
**Write**: "Join us Sunday at 10:30am for worship"

**Instead of**: "All seekers welcome to our sanctuary"
**Write**: "Whether you're just curious or ready to take a next step in faith, you're welcome here"

**Instead of**: "Hopewell UMC extends Christian fellowship"
**Write**: "We'd love to get to know you"
